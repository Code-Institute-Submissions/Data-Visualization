Whiskey Analysis

A scientific meta-analysis of whiskey flavours and quality. All the graphs on this page have used data found here.

Cost rating corelation

The box plot shows the relation between the price of the porudct and the customers reviews. A surprising fact is that as the price of the product goes up the reviews don't rise proportionally to the price increase and the spectrum narrows to a certain minimum level after which the difference doesn't seem to be justified.

Cost to product correlation

This chart showcases the relation between the cost of whiskey and the type. Single Malt Whiskey is the most expensive and the most popular for a number of reasons but the main one is the wide variety of brands and broad spectrum of prices which allows customers to find single malt whiskey for their budget. It is followed by Blend Whiskey and Burbon

World's largest producers

This pie chart presents the top three producers of whiskey in the world. Scotland is the biggest producer of whisky in the world, and has been for at least 100 years with more than 1.2bn bottles exported globally in 2017. It is followed by the USA with a production of approximately 37 million cases of whiskey each year. Canada comes in third place with just over 21 million cases of whisky.

Whiskey count by type

This chart presents the popularity of whiskey based on the type of whiskeys in our data. Malt Whisky ranks among the best whiskies and is predominantly produced in Scotland. It may only be made from malted barley and has to be distilled in pot stills. The process of malting is elaborate and used to be very time-consuming and physically demanding without the modern technology.

Whiskey count by class

This chart presents the popularity of whiskey based on the taste and flavour. As we can see single malt leads by a large margin followed by Burbon and Rye both coming in much closer to each other. One of the reasons Single Malt is the most popular is the large margin of the industry that scotch covers.
